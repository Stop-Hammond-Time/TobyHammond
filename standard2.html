Standard 2: Know the content and how to teach it

2.1 Content and teaching strategies of the teaching area

The image on the left is of some concrete materials I was using with a grade 4 student to develop a better understanding of division. In mathematics, teachers are often too quick to move away from concrete materials (Reys et al., 2020). The right is my high results for the LANTITE, demonstrating my knowledge of numeracy and literacy content.

2.2 Content selection and organisation

Pictured on the left is a brainstorm conducted with a kindergarten class, which served as a formative assessment of students’ knowledge and interests surrounding light. On the right are my notes where I brainstormed topics for further planning based on this assessment.

2.3 Curriculum, assessment and reporting

On the left is the curriculum links used to develop a learning sequence where students design and build their own instruments. On the right is one of the assessment reflections for one of the students who undertook this lesson. I reported on this activity to the parents informally, discussing it with them after school. However, if this was at my workplace, I would have reported by uploading the assessment to Seesaw and including a comment on the student's report.

2.4 Understand and respect Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people to promote reconciliation between Indigenous and non-Indigenous Australians

Pictured is a sample of an essay I wrote for EEE755 where I outlined the history and ongoing impacts of colonisation and issues faced by Indigenous Australians, demonstrating respect and understanding that has helped me promote reconciliation in my teaching.

2.5 Literacy and numeracy strategies

Pictured on the right is a slideshow from a lesson I wrote for mathematics that related to a cooking experience completed in the same week, linking mathematics to real-world experience for better numeracy development. This contrasts with evidence-based strategies for literacy, which favour direct instruction such as those taught with the Decodable Readers Australia materials (pictured right).

2.6 Information and Communication Technology (ICT)

I have made extensive use of ICT in my teaching with slideshows, videos, and using laptops for writing tasks. Pictured is an activity where students learn how to use ICT in addition to ICT being used to enhance their learning. Students play a “choose your own adventure” role-playing game that teaches them how to communicate with generative AI, whilst also working on their literacy.
