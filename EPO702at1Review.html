EPO702 Assignment 1

Introduction

The Anthropocene has been marked by unprecedented and unsustainable changes to our planet and our lives due to human interference (Taylor et al., 2015). Educating students about sustainability is crucial, as highlighted by the cross-curriculum priority set by ACARA (2022). It is essential to prepare future generations to adopt sustainable practices and engage with complex ethical issues (Department of Education and Training [DET], 2019) to ensure the planet remains habitable.

Air pollution is among the top unsustainable practices and is the largest environmental threat to human health, responsible for 1/8th of global deaths (World Health Organisation [WHO], 2024). One of the leading causes of air pollution is inefficient transport (WHO, 2024). The WHO recommends “prioritizing rapid urban transit, walking and cycling networks” as well as switching to less polluting, electric cars (2022).

This makes for an excellent inquiry topic for primary-aged students. Alternative methods of transport are accessible to primary-aged students while human-powered transport can also have a significant positive impact on student health and wellbeing, making it an excellent opportunity to teach children about sustainability. A thorough inquiry would also analyse electric vehicles (EVs), and therefore electricity generation (another incredibly important air pollution topic in itself) which is a particularly important factor in the air pollution generated by EVs.

Additionally, this inquiry topic is also an excellent opportunity for students to learn in and through the arts. In large global issues, children can feel powerless to make positive change. The arts connect the cognitive to the emotional and provide agency for children in these issues. They also challenge children’s understanding of the world (Littledyke, Taylor, & Eames, 2009). Responding to artwork based on these issues is an engaging way for students to engage with the world and issues around them, providing students the opportunity to formulate their own ideas and express them through the arts.

Inquiry Questions

  • What are the effects of air pollution?
  • Where does air pollution come from?
  • How much air pollution do cars create?
  • How efficient are our vehicles? And what are the important factors in their efficiency?
  • What are alternatives to cars? And what stops us from using them?
  • What about electric vehicles?
  • Where does our electricity come from?
