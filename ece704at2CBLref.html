Your Arts/Teaching Practice Focus:

The focus for my practice in this experience is to work on my noticing and responding to studentsâ€™ demonstrations of skill in order to maintain learning that is within the zone of proximal development for each individual.

Educational Dimensions CLIMER/Studio Habits of Mind:

Students CONNECT to a musical classroom culture that is nurtured by participatory dancing. Students develop their drama, dance and pattern LANGUAGE, whilst also learning to negotiate the tableau task with their peers. They explore their IDEAS of pattern and movement whilst MAKING interpretations of pattern pieces and tableaus. Learners RESPOND to the text Pattern Fish, and to the creative dancing of their peers.

Where to next?

This sequence could take on patterns other than repeating patterns, such as growing patterns. Students could also be encouraged to create their own body-based patterns

Adaptations for inclusion:

Students with movement impairments will be included through the inclusion of constraints on all children that are within the abilities of the students with the impairment. Eg. If there is a student (or multiple students) that cannot stand, the educator will ask that all students sit for a pattern, and come up with representations that work whilst seated.
